Constitution – Basics
  • Constitution lays out ideals that form the basis of the kind of country a nation aspires to be.
  • Tradition of Constitution began in: USA (1787)
  • Independence of USA: 4th July 1776
  • India's Constitution: Partly Rigid, Partly Flexible
  • All democratic countries likely have a Constitution — but NOT all countries with a Constitution are democratic.

Constituent Assembly – Timeline
1928
Early draft of Indian Constitution — Nehru Report — submitted by All Parties Conference at Lucknow, chaired by Motilal Nehru.
1934
M. N. Roy first proposed the idea of a Constituent Assembly for India.
1935
Indian National Congress officially demanded a Constituent Assembly.
1940 – August Offer
Proposed by Lord Linlithgow (Viceroy) on 8 Aug 1940 to gain Indian support in WWII. Promised Dominion Status after the war. REJECTED
1942 – Cripps Proposal
Offered Dominion Status + Constituent Assembly to draft new constitution. REJECTED
1945 – Wavell Plan
Also called Shimla Conference. Proposed by Lord Wavell. Interim Executive Council with equal representation for Hindus and Muslims. REJECTED
1946 – Cabinet Mission Plan
ACCEPTED by both Congress and Muslim League → Constituent Assembly was formed based on this plan.
Members: Pethick-Lawrence (Chairman), Stafford Cripps, A.V. Alexander
"Dominion status is dead as a door nail." — Jawaharlal Nehru (on why the first 3 British proposals were rejected)
All three plans (August Offer, Cripps, Wavell) were rejected because Congress demanded Purna Swaraj while British only offered Dominion Status.

Functions & Working of Constituent Assembly
Functions
  • Law-making body (legislate) → G. V. Mavalankar (1st Speaker, Lok Sabha)
  • Constitution-making body
  • Functioned as First Parliament of India
  • Adopted National Flag → 22 July 1947
  • National Anthem: "Jana Gana Mana" (Rabindranath Tagore) → 24 Jan 1950
  • National Song: "Vande Mataram" (Bankim Chandra Chatterjee) → 24 Jan 1950
  • Ratified India's Commonwealth membership → May 1949
  • Elected Dr. Rajendra Prasad as 1st President → 26 Jan 1950
Working
  • 1st Meeting: 9 Dec 1946 (211 members present)
  • Temporary President: Dr. Sachchidanand Sinha
  • Permanent President: Dr. Rajendra Prasad
  • Vice President: H. C. Mukherjee
